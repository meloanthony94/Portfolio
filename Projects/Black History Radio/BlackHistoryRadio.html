Overview
Black History Month project
Using a vintage radio as a vessel to tell stories from historical figures in black history
Instead of tuning into a radio station, users tune into a specifc year
Each year is dedicated to a historical figure in black history
multiple audio clips per year to add replayability
Paired with a custom guide made by artist Lauren Heyen as a companion piece
Designed after real radio guides from the 1960s
Vintage Radio
Needed a vintage radio of satisfy the creative intent
Wanted to user to feel like they were transported back in time
vintage radio would help put the user in that mindset
However, modern radio would save me time because I can reuse the components.
Only had a couple weeks to put it all together
Used a vintage style but modern radio. Saved me enough time to complete the project on time.
Replaced the frequency display sticker with a custom made display representing 6 dates in black history

Historical Figures
From the start of the project i knew i wanted an equal number of both make and female figures of black history
The final list contains 6 total figues spanning from 1947 - 2008
Each figure has 2-3 clips in an effort to add replayability and make the radio feel more dynamic
Aside from they year, the project was missing a way for users to know who they were listening too
That requirement led to the creation of the companion art peice of a 1960s inspired news paper page promoting the Black History Radio program
art peice was created by the talented Lauren Heyen

Sony Spresense
To make the project come to life, i would require lots of audio at the highest quality possible and fastest playback
Need to change between radio stations with ease
Wanted to avoid an amp if I could
These technical requirements led me to the excellent Sony Spresense micro controller.
The Spresense supports 192 kHz / 24-bit audio with a built in amp and SD card slot.
This enabled me to avoid including any other hardware, soley using the Spresense
I came out of this project impressed with the Spresense and look forward to trying it on camera based projects in the future

Artist Showcase
Black History Radio was exhibited at the Black History Artist Showcase at Universal Creative
Was amongst a variety of other amazing art peieces by some truly talented artist
Learned that users are not naturally inclined to touch or play with items designated as art.
This led to the "Try Me" label on the radio to encourage people to come up and interact with the radio
